About this sequence

The Fibonacci sequence is a series of numbers where each number is the sum of the two preceding ones, starting from 0 and 1. It is named after the Italian mathematician Leonardo of Pisa, known as Fibonacci, who introduced the sequence to Western mathematics in his 1202 book Liber Abaci.

The sequence appears in nature, such as in the arrangement of leaves, the branching of trees, and the spirals of shells. The Fibonacci sequence is closely related to the golden ratio, an important concept in art, architecture, and nature, as the ratio of consecutive Fibonacci numbers approaches the golden ratio as the sequence progresses
